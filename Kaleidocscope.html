The Science of Symmetry

A kaleidoscope is an optical instrument that uses mirrors to create complex, symmetrical patterns from a collection of small, colorful objects. The magic comes from the principle of **multiple reflections**. Light from the objects bounces between the angled mirrors, creating virtual images that your eye perceives as an intricate, repeating design.

The Maker's Workflow

1

Prepare the Mirrors

Measure and cut three identical mirror strips that will fit snugly inside your cardboard tube when formed into a triangle.

2

Form the Prism

Tape the three mirror strips together along their long edges to create a triangular prism. This is the core of your kaleidoscope.

3

Create the Object Cell

At one end of the tube, create a small, sealed chamber using plastic wrap. Place your colorful beads inside this chamber.

4

Assemble & View

Slide the mirror prism into the tube, secure the ends, and create an eyepiece. Look through and enjoy the show!

The Magic of Multiple Mirrors

The number of mirrors you use dramatically changes the pattern you see. A standard three-mirror kaleidoscope creates a complex, repeating pattern, while using only two mirrors produces a different kind of circular symmetry. This chart compares the perceived complexity of the patterns.
