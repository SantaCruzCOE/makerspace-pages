Level Up Your Grad Cap

Combine 2D vinyl cutting and 3D printing to create a one-of-a-kind decoration that celebrates your achievement.

Two Technologies, One Project

This project introduces two core methods of **digital fabrication**. You'll use a subtractive tool that cuts material away and an additive tool that builds material up, layer by layer.

The Digital Fabrication Workflow

Part 1: The Vinyl Layer

1. Measure your cap and create your graphic in Cricut Design Space.

2. Send the design to the Cricut to cut the vinyl precisely.

3. Weed the excess vinyl and apply transfer tape over your design.

4. Clean the cap surface and carefully apply the vinyl design.

Part 2: The 3D Element

1. Find or design a 3D model (e.g., in Tinkercad).

2. Slice the model in software like Bambu Studio to prepare it for printing.

3. Send the file to the 3D printer and start the build process.

4. Once cool, remove the finished object from the build plate.

Part 3: Final Assembly

Use double-sided tape or hot glue to attach your 3D element onto your vinyl design for a stunning, multi-layered effect.

Choosing the Right Tool

Vinyl cutting and 3D printing have different strengths. Vinyl is fast and ideal for detailed 2D graphics, while 3D printing excels at creating complex, structural 3D objects. This chart compares them across key fabrication metrics.
