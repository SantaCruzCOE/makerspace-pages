Digital Design, Physical Form

Learn the fundamentals of digital fabrication by designing and creating a custom cardstock costume for your phone with a Cricut Maker.

The Big Idea: From Flat to Form

This project is all about turning a 2D design into a 3D object. You will design a flat pattern, known as a **net diagram**, which includes precise cuts and fold lines. When assembled, this net transforms into a three-dimensional costume that fits your phone perfectly.

The Digital Fabrication Workflow

1

Measure & Sketch

Precisely measure your phone's length, width, and thickness. Sketch a rough net diagram on paper to plan your design.

2

Digital Design

Recreate your net diagram in Cricut Design Space. Use your exact measurements for the base and sides, and add score lines for folds.

3

Cut & Score

Load cardstock into the Cricut. The machine will first score the fold lines and then cut the perimeter of your design with high precision.

4

Assemble & Decorate

Carefully fold along the scored lines and glue the tabs to create the 3D form. Test the fit, then add creative details to bring your character to life.

Why Precision Matters

In digital fabrication, accuracy is everything. Even a tiny measurement error of one millimeter can be the difference between a costume that fits perfectly and one that is too loose or too tight. The Cricut's precision ensures your digital design is translated to the physical world accurately.
