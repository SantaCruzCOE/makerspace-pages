The Paper Bridge Challenge

Explore the fundamental principles of structural engineering by building a suspension bridge from simple materials.

The Science of Strength

A suspension bridge works by masterfully balancing two key forces: **tension** and **compression**. The weight on the bridge (the load) is transferred through cables under tension to towers, which are then put under compression, directing the force safely to the ground.

The Maker's Blueprint

1

Create the Bridge Deck

Fold a sheet of paper accordion-style lengthwise. This corrugation adds significant rigidity and strength.

2

Build the Towers

Roll several sheets of paper into tight, dense tubes to act as the vertical supports for your bridge.

3

Run the Main Cables

Drape two long pieces of string over the towers, allowing them to sag in the middle. Anchor the ends securely.

4

Attach the Hangers

Use shorter strings to connect the deck to the main cables. These hangers transfer the load upwards.

5

Test & Refine

Place your bridge over a gap and begin adding weights. Observe where it fails and reinforce your design.

Data-Driven Design

Engineering is all about optimizing design based on data. Taller towers allow for a deeper, stronger cable curve, significantly increasing the load your bridge can handle. Similarly, stronger materials for the deck make a huge difference.
