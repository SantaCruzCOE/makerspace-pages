The Science Behind the Scribble

Unbalanced Force

An off-center weight (a cork!) on a spinning motor creates a powerful vibration. This kinetic energy is what makes the bot shake, wiggle, and draw.

Step-by-Step Build Guide

1

Motor Prep

Attach a wire to each motor terminal. Then, firmly push the cork onto the motor's spinning shaft. Make sure it's off-center to create the wobble!

2

Create the Switch

On your cardboard base, push in two thumbtacks. Attach a paperclip to one so it can pivot. This will be your on/off switch to complete the circuit.

3

Assemble the Body

Hot glue the motor to the top of your cardboard. Flip it over and glue the three markers to the bottom, forming a stable tripod for the bot to stand on.

4

Wire It Up!

Connect a wire from the battery pack to one thumbtack. Connect one motor wire to the other thumbtack. Finally, connect the last motor wire directly to the other battery pack wire.

GO

Get Scribbling!

Place your bot on a large piece of paper, take the caps off the markers, and pivot the paperclip switch to touch the second thumbtack. Watch your creation come to life!
