The Maker's Workflow

1

Research & Design

Choose a bird, study its diet, and sketch a design for its beak, noting the key features that make it effective.

2

Build Your Beak

Create your design using either cardboard for rapid prototyping or Tinkercad for a detailed 3D printed model.

3

Test Your Beak

Set up stations with different "food" items (like rice, pasta, or gummy worms) and see how well your beak can pick them up.

4

Analyze & Iterate

Compare your results. How well did your specialized beak perform? How could you improve the design?

Data-Driven Design: Beak Efficiency

This chart shows how different beak designs might perform when tested on various food types. A specialized beak, like a finch's, is highly efficient for its target food (seeds) but performs poorly on others, illustrating the concept of adaptation.
